### **PHASE 4: The Risk & Blindspot Backlog (Do Not Solve Now)**

_These are the critical "Unknown Unknowns." Do not try to figure these out today. Add them as specific tickets in your project management software (Trello, Jira, Asana) to be researched and mitigated during the system design phase._

**1\. The PII & Data Privacy Liability (Compliance)**

- _The Risk:_ Xactimate PDFs contain highly sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (PII)—names, phone numbers, addresses.
- _The Backlog Ticket:_ Research SOC2 compliance and "Zero-Data Retention" agreements for LLM APIs (like OpenAI Enterprise or Anthropic). We must legally guarantee contractors that their clients' data is never used to train public AI models.

**2\. The API Paywall Trap**

- _The Risk:_ We build a beautiful integration, but the CRM blocks it.
- _The Backlog Ticket:_ Do our target CRMs (JobNimbus, AccuLynx) require the contractor to be on their most expensive "Enterprise" subscription tier just to generate an API key? If yes, this significantly shrinks our Total Addressable Market (TAM) to only large companies. We must audit CRM pricing pages.

**3\. The Context Window / Token Limit Drop-off**

- _The Risk:_ A standard residential roof estimate is 15 pages. A commercial hotel fire estimate is 200+ pages. LLMs have "token limits" and can suffer from "lost in the middle" hallucinations where they forget math in the middle of a massive document.
- _The Backlog Ticket:_ Architect a PDF "chunking" strategy (e.g., parsing the document 5 pages at a time and aggregating the JSON output at the end) and calculate the API cost-per-page to ensure our pricing model is profitable.

**4\. The "Tax & Overhead" Collision**

- _The Risk:_ Xactimate applies 10% Overhead & 10% Profit (O\&P), plus local tax based on zip code, at the very _bottom_ of the grand total. CRMs also calculate tax automatically.
- _The Backlog Ticket:_ Determine how destination CRMs handle overarching taxes vs. line-item taxes. We must architect a math rule to prevent the CRM from double-taxing the imported material order.

**5\. The F9 Note Contradiction (The Logic Loop)**

- _The Risk:_ What if the structured line item code says `30 SQ` of shingles, but the adjuster typed an unstructured F9 note underneath saying _"Note: Only replace the back slope (15 SQ)"_? How does the system weigh the contradiction?
- _The Backlog Ticket:_ Establish strict "Human-in-the-Loop" UI protocols. The AI must be trained to _never_ override math automatically; it must highlight contradictions in red, forcing the human user to manually select the correct quantity before syncing.
